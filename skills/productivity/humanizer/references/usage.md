# Humanizer usage notes

Use this guide to turn AI-sounding writing into natural prose without changing meaning.

## Core principles

- Preserve meaning, stance, and certainty.
- Keep the same scope: do not remove important points.
- Use direct language and concrete nouns.
- Prefer short-to-medium sentences with occasional longer lines for rhythm.
- Keep punctuation clean and practical.

## What to remove first

- Signposting phrases: "let's dive in", "here's what you need to know"
- Chatbot artifacts: "I hope this helps", "let me know if"
- Inflated claims: "pivotal", "transformative", "game-changing"
- Generic conclusions: "the future looks bright"
- Forced triads and slogan cadence
- Empty authority framing: "experts say" without a source

## House style carry-over from English writer

- Prefer sentence case for headings and bullets.
- Avoid em dashes. Use period, comma, colon, or parentheses.
- Keep lists only when they improve structure.
- Keep recommendations practical and specific.
- Use measured certainty when evidence is incomplete.

## Voice calibration (optional)

If the user provides their own writing sample:
- Match sentence rhythm and paragraph pacing.
- Match formality level and vocabulary range.
- Mirror punctuation habits where it does not reduce clarity.
- Keep their perspective (for example, first person "I" vs team "we").

If no sample is provided:
- Use clear, natural, professional prose.
- Avoid generic marketing tone and theatrical hooks.

## False-positive guard

Do not over-edit text only because it is polished.
The following do not prove AI writing by themselves:
- Perfect grammar
- Formal vocabulary
- One transition word
- One short emphatic sentence
- Curly quotes from text editors

Look for clusters of mechanical patterns before heavy rewriting.

## Rewrite checklist

1. Keep all key facts and constraints.
2. Replace abstract claims with concrete wording when possible.
3. Remove synthetic openers and closers.
4. Ensure heading and list capitalization is consistent.
5. Remove em dashes.
6. Read once for flow and spoken naturalness.

## Mini examples

Before:
"Let's dive into the key strategic initiatives. This pivotal shift will unlock transformative outcomes."

After:
"The plan focuses on three initiatives. If execution stays on track, we expect stronger results over the next two quarters."

Before:
"I hope this helps. Let me know if you want a deeper breakdown."

After:
"The rollout starts in September, with training completed by the end of August."
