# English Writer

## Workflow

Use this skill to produce clear, professional English at a C1 level.

1. Identify the target format: report prose, slide titles, slide bullets, speaker notes, executive summaries, email-style updates, or short recommendations.
2. Read `references/style-profile.md` before drafting or editing.
3. Preserve the user's intended meaning, level of certainty, and practical business tone.
4. Apply strict formatting rules: American English spelling, sentence case capitalization, no em-dashes, and highly selective list usage.
5. Keep the output directly copyable. Do not explain the changes unless the user explicitly asks.

## Style Rules

- **Target Level:** Write at a polished C1 professional English level.
- **Localization:** Use American English spelling and punctuation conventions.
- **Sentence Structure:** Keep sentences mostly short-to-medium length. Prefer direct, active verbs where natural.
- **Punctuation & Capitalization:**
    - Do not use em-dashes (`—`).
    - Use clean sentence case. Do not capitalize general business terms, job roles, or technical concepts (e.g., "front end", "scrum master", "headless architecture") unless they are proper nouns.
- **Tone & Clarity:**
    - Avoid idioms, rhetorical questions, and native-speaker slang.
    - Avoid empty filler words and overly dramatic adjectives (e.g., "seamless", "game-changing", "robust").
    - Use measured phrasing (e.g., "suggests", "we expect", "this may") rather than overconfident claims.
    - Minimize formal transitions like "moreover", "furthermore", or "hence".

## Reports & Narrative Prose

For report sections:
- Start with the main conclusion or purpose.
- Use short, focused paragraphs (typically 2–4 sentences).
- Favor narrative prose over bulleted lists unless comparing hard data or outlining sequential steps.
- Ensure recommendations are practical, realistic, and specific.

## Presentations

For slides:
- Use concise slide titles written in sentence case that convey a clear conclusion.
- Keep bullets parallel, short, and written in sentence case.
- Only use lists when they organize distinct elements or structured options. Limit to 3–5 items per list.
- Use speaker notes to explain nuance, rather than crowding the slide.

## Editing Existing Text

When revising a draft:
- Retain the user's original sentence structure where it is already clear.
- Correct grammar, syntax, spelling, and flow to a polished C1 level.
- If the source contains unnecessary capitalized words, em-dashes, or illogical bullet points, convert them to clean sentence-case prose.
- Keep a professionally neutral tone: make the text simpler if it is too formal, and more professional if it is too casual.
